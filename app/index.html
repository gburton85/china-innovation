Be an innovator

Anyone can be an innovator

Innovation skills are needed by all types of organisations all over the world. Developing these skills will help you to get ahead in your career and boost your employability, no matter what you want to do.

Being an innovator is also now more possible than ever before.

China in particular is very supportive of innovation and entrepreneurs and being at university is a great time to develop these skills.

So whether you’re looking to develop your innovation skills, you have an idea for a business, or you have an existing business and are looking for it to grow, use this website to give yourself a headstart, learn something new and connect with people to grow yourself and your innovation skills.

  • Case Study: WMG

    Being innovative starts with understanding “value” and its relationship with customers. It starts with understanding what the real assets are that a business has to build upon. These can be physical assets (for example, buildings, machinery, delivery vehicles) or virtual assets (for example, relationships, business reputation). Understanding your business assets means you can leverage these to produce value. For China Metals is was the realisation that their core asset was their ability to bend and join wire into complex shapes, and that this asset could be used in a range of different sectors.
