3. Win:Win

One of the most important skills of being an innovator is ensuring that you create a win:win situation.

You are driving along in your car on a wild, stormy night. You pass by a bus stop, and you see three people waiting for the bus:

  • An old lady who looks as if she is about to die.

  • An old friend who once saved your life.

  • The perfect man (or) woman you have been dreaming about.

There is only room for one passenger in your car. What do you do? You could pick up the old lady, because she is going to die, and thus you should save her first; or you could take the old friend because he once saved your life, and this would be the perfect chance to pay him back. However, you may never be able to find your perfect dream lover again.

A business exists in an eco-system and has stakeholders (customers, suppliers, shareholders). For a business to run smoothly, it needs to keep all its stakeholders happy. Businesses need to revisit their model at regular intervals in order to create a continuous win-win situation for everyone involved.

Top tip

Understanding what people really value can help you to create a business. The owners of the black sock business quickly realised that the value of their business lay not in how their black socks reached their customers, but in the removal of customers having to think about buying socks.

This approach can be expanded into many other areas, for example razor blades for shaving and ink cartridges for your printer.
